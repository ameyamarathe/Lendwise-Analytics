# Lendwise Analytics Platform

An end-to-end analytics engineering project simulating the data platform of a 
UK consumer credit lender. Built using Microsoft Fabric, dbt, Power BI, 
and Python, with full Git version control.

This project is part of me preparing for my DP600 & DP700 Fabric certification.
---

## The scenario

Lendwise is a simulation of a  UK consumer credit company offering personal loans, 
debt consolidation, and credit-builder products. I am designing and building the platform that turns
raw operational data into trustworthy, governed datasets that the business can use for credit risk 
analysis, regulatory reporting, and operational decision-making.

---

## Business questions the platform answers

- What is our default rate, broken down by product, credit band, and origination cohort?
- How does customer payment behaviour change over the life of a loan?
- What does the IFRS 9 staging breakdown look like across the portfolio?
- Which customer segments drive the most originations, and at what risk?
- How is portfolio quality trending month over month?

---

## Architecture

A medallion architecture in Microsoft Fabric:

**Bronze (raw):** Synthetic CSVs landed into a Lakehouse — customers, 
applications, loans, payments, products, arrears snapshots.

**Silver (cleaned, conformed):** Validated, typed, deduplicated. Slowly 
Changing Dimension Type 2 logic applied to customer attributes (income, 
employment, address). Built using PySpark notebooks and dbt models.

**Gold (analytics-ready):** Star schema designed for BI consumption.
- Dimensions: `dim_customer`, `dim_product`, `dim_date`, `dim_loan`
- Facts: `fact_originations`, `fact_repayments`, `fact_arrears_snapshot`

**Semantic layer:** Power BI Direct Lake model with measures for default rate, 
arrears %, expected credit loss, vintage curves, and portfolio KPIs.

**Reports:** Three focused dashboards — Portfolio Health, Credit Risk, 
Originations.

## Data

All data is synthetic, generated by `scripts/generate_lendwise_data.py`. The 
generator produces ~80,000 customers, ~45,000 applications, ~22,000 loans, 
~1,000,000 payments, and 24 months of month-end arrears snapshots, with realistic 
UK postcodes, income distributions, and credit-band-driven default behaviour.

No real customer data is used or referenced anywhere in this repository.

The generator is informed by:
- The Home Credit Default Risk schema (Kaggle) — for column patterns
- Bank of England and UK Finance public statistics — for realistic aggregate ratios
- UK regulatory terminology (FCA CONC, IFRS 9 staging)
